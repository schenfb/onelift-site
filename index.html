How It Works

1

Pick up the dumbbell

Five exercises, one per day, rotating. Tap a name to watch how it's done:

  • Bulgarian Split Squatlower push
  • Single-Arm Pressupper push (vertical)
  • Single-Leg Deadliftlower pull
  • Single-Arm Rowupper pull
  • Single-Arm Bench Pressupper push (horizontal)
2

Do 5 reps, 3 sets per side

Weak side first. Same weight for all sets. Rest 2-3 minutes between sets. The whole thing takes about 20 minutes.

3

Add a little weight next time

The app suggests your next weight. You confirm based on what your dumbbell actually has. Small increments, consistently, over months.

4

Hit a wall? Deload and rebuild

When it gets too heavy, step back 4 weeks of progress and climb again. You'll pass your old number and keep going. This is the cycle: load, deload, reload.

The System

EquipmentOne adjustable dumbbell + a chair or bench
Exercises5 single-arm/single-leg compound movements
Daily volume5 reps × 6 sets (3 per side)
Frequency1 exercise per day, 5-day rotation
ProgressionAdd weight when all sets are complete
DeloadGo back 4 weeks when you hit a wall
Time~20 minutes per session
CostFree.

Who This Isn't For

I always mark the boundaries honestly. This system is not built for you if:

  • You want to become a bodybuilder or chase maximum hypertrophy — that needs higher volume and a more complex program. Take another road.
  • You're already an intermediate barbell lifter past linear progression — you need periodization, not this.
  • You want fancy variety and a new routine every week — this is deliberately monotonous. It works through consistency, not novelty.
  • You won't log one line after each session — without records, the whole system falls apart.

But if you just want to get stronger at home, with the lowest possible friction, steadily, for years — this was built for you.
